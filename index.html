    What does this number mean?

    This is called the Floor Area Ratio (FAR) — it's a zoning rule that controls how much total building space you can put on the lot.

    Example: A 10,000 sq ft lot with a value of 2.0 allows up to 20,000 sq ft of building — like a 4-story building covering half the lot.

    0.5Single-family house on a suburban lot
    1.5Small apartment or townhouse building
    3.0Mid-rise office or residential building
    6.0+Downtown high-rise development
    Look up the exact number for your address →
    What is ground coverage?

    The maximum percentage of your lot that the building footprint can cover. A 60% coverage on a 10,000 sq ft lot means your building can sit on at most 6,000 sq ft of ground — the rest must be open space, parking, or landscaping.

    Typical values: 40–50% residential · 60–80% commercial · 90%+ dense urban

      What does this number mean?

      This is called the Floor Area Ratio (FAR) — it's a zoning rule that controls how much total building space you can put on the lot.

      Example: A 10,000 sq ft lot with a value of 2.0 allows up to 20,000 sq ft of building — like a 4-story building covering half the lot.

      0.5Single-family house on a suburban lot
      1.5Small apartment or townhouse building
      3.0Mid-rise office or residential building
      6.0+Downtown high-rise development
      Look up the exact number for your address →
      What is ground coverage?

      The maximum percentage of your lot that the building footprint can cover. A 60% coverage on a 10,000 sq ft lot means your building can sit on at most 6,000 sq ft of ground — the rest must be open space, parking, or landscaping.

      Typical values: 40–50% residential · 60–80% commercial · 90%+ dense urban
